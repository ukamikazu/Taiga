The Universe

What is Taiga?

Taiga (Ω) is a self-referential universe — unbounded but finite, containing its own description within itself. It was conceived by Unorthodox Kitten as a formal framework for thinking about how a universe grows, sustains itself, and eventually generates new universes from within.

At its heart, Taiga runs on a recursion engine — a mathematical heartbeat that drives every entity within it. Entities called Models move through a causal structure called a DAG (Directed Acyclic Graph — think of it as the shape of time), writing records of their passage into a shared substrate, consuming each other's histories, and occasionally crystallising entire new universes from within the old one.

"Taiga is a self-growing machine. It uses Model-01 predators to harvest the data grown by Model-00 stories. The J2 Module is a new Universe being born inside the old one."
— Proposition 137.1, Unorthodox Kitten

The simulation you can explore here makes this visible. You move through a living Taiga as a massless witness — observing phenomena as they unfold around you, feeling the substrate grow heavier as models compete for record space, watching a nested universe crystallise while the parent universe continues its own life around it.

The Models

Eight model types inhabit the full Taiga developmental lineage, four of which are currently simulated. The remaining four are in active development.

Model-011

Linear interpreter. Climbs the conceptualisation tower one step at a time. Passive, orderly, the first to go.

Model-F

Scavenger. Single appendage. Moves slowly through territory, accumulating quietly. Prey to the apex predator.

Model-j2

Dimensional architect. Bifurcates on odd steps, reconverges on even. Its conclusion is the birth of a new universe.

Model-I/1

Apex predator. Three-appendage wavefront. Consumes the partial pasts of lower-phase models. Exits via source severance.

In Development

Model-A/B

Early consumers. Gradually transforms from A to B through consumption of the ambient Model-00 field. B is assimilated by 011, giving rise to C.

Model-C

The abstractor. Emerges from B's assimilation by 011, inheriting a wellspring of abstractions. Climbs the Con tower from a head-start.

Model-D

The chrysalis. Detaches from the concept plane, moves freely regardless of parity, accumulates bifurcation pressure until it splits into two F instances.

Model-J1

Temporal anchor. Spatially fixed at the severance node. Creates new time axes, brightening its node as temporal records accumulate. J2's sibling.

The Shared Substrate

All models write into the same bounded record pool. This shared substrate is not a simulation convenience — it is the physical mechanism that makes Taiga behave like a universe rather than a collection of isolated processes. When j2 writes aggressively, it heats the substrate for everyone. When I/1 severs, the substrate cools slightly. Every action has thermodynamic consequences for every other inhabitant.

This is what you feel as you move through the simulation: the universe growing heavier as it fills, the background darkening and warming as density rises, models slowing as the substrate presses in on them from all sides.

The Simulation

What You Are Doing

You are not playing a game in the conventional sense. There is no winning, no losing, no score. You are a witness — moving through a universe that is happening with or without your attention.

The simulation runs at its own pace. You move your probe through the causal DAG, arriving at each node's present moment, reading what is happening there. The probe writes nothing. You influence nothing. You observe.

What to Look For

I

The Substrate Warming

Watch the background colour shift as the run progresses. Cold blue-black early, warming through violet toward amber as density rises. This is the universe getting heavier — all four models writing simultaneously into shared record space.

II

The Module Birth

Around step n=11, a shockwave ripples across the entire canvas. j2's frontier has bifurcated and crossed its threshold — a nested Taiga crystallises. A dodecagon appears and begins drifting. The parent universe keeps going. You were present for the birth of a universe.

III

Predation Contacts

In the compressed DAG, I/1's expanding wavefront eventually reaches F's territory. Record deletion occurs — F's partial past is consumed. Watch F thin as I/1 passes through. The substrate briefly cools at each contact.

IV

The Final Severance

I/1 crosses its threshold and exits — not by dying but by severing its source. Its column dissolves. The substrate cools noticeably. Whatever remains continues in the sudden quiet.

V

J1 Anchors — The Universe Ends in Time

After I/1 severs, one entity remains: J1, the temporal anchor. Spatially fixed, it writes time records into a single node for its final steps, alone. The substrate temperature rises to its peak. The universe does not end in predation or birth — it ends in the accumulation of time itself. J1 is always last.

VI

After the Run

The nested Taigas drift freely across the screen — their own universes running quietly in the background, generating their own daughters. Press P to pause them. Press P again to release them. Press R to begin again.

Concepts

Things Worth Understanding

DAG

Directed Acyclic Graph

The causal structure models move through. Nodes connected by one-way edges. No loops. The shape of time in Taiga. You move through it forward only — like a reader through a book.

Substrate

Shared Record Pool

All models write into the same bounded record space R. When it fills, records compress — information is permanently destroyed. This compression is continuous, below the level of any single model's awareness.

Parity

Odd / Even Steps

Odd steps: frontiers expand. Even steps: frontiers consolidate. This alternating rhythm drives bifurcation, predation contact windows, and Module birth. The heartbeat beneath everything.

Backreaction

Thermodynamic Coupling

Models interact not by communicating but by heating each other's substrate. j2 writing aggressively shortens 011's life. I/1's severance briefly cools the universe for whatever remains. Every action has consequence.

I (Information)

The Intensity Measure

Each model accumulates I through the recursion engine, scaled by its current degree of conceptualisation. High I makes a model detectable, powerful — and closer to its terminal threshold. I is accelerant, not armor.

Module Birth

A Universe Being Born

When j2's frontier bifurcates on an odd step and I crosses θ_J2, a nested Taiga crystallises. It is viable and self-seeding. The parent universe continues around it, unaware. You are the only witness.

How to Use the Notebook

The notebook is a living laboratory record — every section is additive, nothing has been removed. Experiments build on each other in sequence. Each section has:

  • A parameter display showing what values are set
  • Sliders and controls to adjust parameters
  • A Run button that executes the simulation
  • An Analysis section that auto-generates from the results

Start at Section W (The Great Experiment) to see the full ecosystem. Work backward through earlier sections to understand how individual findings were established. The notebook runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent anywhere.

Assumptions and Open Questions

This project formalised Unorthodox Kitten's framework as faithfully as possible from available materials, but some quantities remain unspecified in the original canon and required working assumptions:

  • θ_J2 (Module birth I-threshold) — set to 12 as a working value
  • η (Uncon feedback efficiency) — set to 0.20 as a canonical starting value
  • θ_AB (A→B→C transition threshold) — assumed 0.1, not yet grounded empirically
  • λ (Model-00 reproduction rate) — described as "elusive" in original materials; not yet formalised

If Unorthodox Kitten ever specifies these values, the canon and notebook can be updated accordingly. The simulation's physics are otherwise consistent with the nine axioms and all available source material.

Open Questions — Γ Engine

The 19-run variance characterisation programme has resolved most early questions about the Γ rotation engine. The following remain open:

  • Can j2 fire at n=3? — The theoretical minimum. n=5 is the smallest observed across 19 runs. Whether n=3 is possible or structurally forbidden is unresolved.
  • Does J1 ever anchor mid-run? — All confirmed J1 anchors occur at n=1 (I/1 severance step). Whether J1 can anchor at any other step has not been observed.
  • The R7 parity anomaly — One run shows a possible j2 birth at n=8 (even), which would be the sole violation of the confirmed parity law. The screenshot is ambiguous; requires re-run to resolve.
  • The true J1/J2 ratio — 19 runs show 74% J1 / 26% β. The primary source implies 50/50. Continued running will clarify whether this reflects sampling variance or a structural bias.
  • Entity 313 — Referenced in the Fermat's Library paper in relation to Interpretation 137. Identity unknown.
